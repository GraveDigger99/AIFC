Second Party Opinion

Sustainable financial instruments are specifically designed to fund projects and activities that have positive environmental or social impacts, alongside generating financial returns for investors. They cover a wide range of instruments, such as green, social, sustainability, sustainability-linked bonds, loans, equity, and all the specific thematic instruments within those wider categories, such as blue, transition, KPI-linked, SDG-linked bonds and loans, green equity etc.

However, external review of sustainable finance instruments, which typically involves third-party assessments or certifications, are often required by investors, lenders and stock exchanges to verify the environmental or social claims associated with these products. External review plays a crucial role in promoting transparency, credibility, and accountability within the sustainable finance sector, thereby fostering trust and confidence among investors and stakeholders.

Second Party Opinion

An institution or consultant with environmental/social/sustainability expertise that is independent from the issuer/borrower may provide a so called Second Party Opinion on the alignment of the issuer’s or borrower’s green/social/sustainability/sustainability-linked finance framework or climate transition strategy with recognised international guidelines (such as ICMA Green bonds principles etc.)
